<p><p>Carbon nanotubes (CNTs) are cylindrical nanostructures made of carbon atoms, exhibiting exceptional mechanical, electrical, and thermal properties. The market for CNTs is primarily divided into two types: single-walled nanotubes (SWNTs) and multi-walled nanotubes (MWNTs). SWNTs consist of a single layer of carbon atoms arranged in a seamless cylinder, offering remarkable conductivity and versatility. In contrast, MWNTs comprise multiple concentric layers, providing enhanced strength and stability. Both types have diverse applications spanning electronics, materials science, and biomedical fields.</p></p>
